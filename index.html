Intro

Xavier is an undergraduate student in Nanyang Technology University, Singapore, pursueing a Bachelor of Social Sciences (Honours) in Psychology.

Recently graduating with a Diploma (with Merit) in Psychology Studies and a Specialist Diploma in Gerontology (Optimal Ageing Practice) from Temasek Polytechnic, School of Humanities & Social Sciences, Xavier has been actively applying his knowledge in the fields of clinical psychology, industrial/ organisational psychology, and defence psychology. An avid scientist, Xavier explores advanced statistical methodologies as applied in psychological science in his downtime.

Intrigued by neuro-developmental disorders and his past experiences with clinical disorders, Xavier is interested in specialising in clinical neuropsychology. His research interests lie in participatory arts & well-being, participatory action research, and clinical psychology.

Outside of his academic life, Xavier unwinds by playing the piano and violin, exploring texts published by Oliver Sacks, and watching anime [His all-time favourite is 'Your Lie in April' ;)]!

Publications

Enhancing the caregiving experience of family care partners in Singapore through an arts programme for persons with dementia: An exploratory study

Authors: Wan Jie Tan, Xavier Lim*, Theresa Lee, Sze Chi Wong, Hwan Jing Koh, & Donald Yeo
Published on Aging & Mental Health, 2022

This study sought to broaden the sociocultural perspectives of arts-based programmes by investigating the impact of a locally developed 'Arts & Dementia' programme for persons living with dementia and their family care partners in Singapore. Findings highlighted potential benefits of community-based arts activities in enabling caregiving to be a more positive experience for care partners.

The impact of the Arts and Dementia Program on short‐term well‐being in older persons with dementia from Singapore

Authors: Wan Jie Tan, Xavier Lim*, Theresa Lee, Sze Chi Wong, Hwan Jing Koh, & Donald Yeo
Published on Australasian Journal on Ageing, 2022

This study explored the impact of a locally developed 'Arts & Dementia' program on the short-term well-being of older persons living with dementia in Singapore. Overall findings suggest activities that promote reminiscence and leisure are strongly associated with enhanced well-being in persons living with dementia.

Writings

Finding Solace in Music Through the Lens of "Dear Evan Hansen"

Published on Singapore Psychologist #10: Okayness, 2022

Recently, Dear Evan Hansen, a Broadway musical about the intricacies of living with the desire to belong has garnered remarkable commendation in the United States. Through insights from psychology and musicology, this article attempts to explain why some of us find solace in music, through the lens of Dear Evan Hansen.

Participatory Arts

Published on Dementia Hub Singapore, 2021

In an attempt to reduce decline in cognitive and social skills associated with the onset of dementia, dementia specialists have expanded from the roots of biomedical treatment to explore other innovative interventions. This article explores one of such non-pharmacological interventions - participatory arts.

Artificial Consciousness: Can Machines Ever Become Conscious?

Published on Singapore Psychologist #9: Human-Machine Interactions, 2021

Contemporary entertainment industries have populated the theatrical and gaming landscapes with fictional tales exploring futuristic accounts of humans living in tandem with self-aware robots and sentient machines. This article examines the feasibility of such depictions - can machines ever become conscious, and how do we truly determine if a machine is intelligent?

The Search for Animal Sentience: Do Nonhuman Animals Have a Theory of Mind?

Published on Singapore Psychologist #8: Human-Animal Interactions, 2021

Are humans alone in their ability to experience and reason on the basis on unobservable mental states? This article supplements a primer on the theory of mind before confronting contenous questions posed on the nature of animal sentience.

Shinrin-Yoku: Leveraging Nature as a Therapeutic Medium

Published on Singapore Psychologist #7: Human-Environment Interactions, 2021

The harmonious relationship between humans and nature is often considered one of the core characteristics of Japanese culture. This article consolidates and critiques one of such novel practice - Shinrin Yoku (literally, taking in or bathing in the forest atmosphere) - and raises its potential benefits on physiological and psychology well-being.

Cultivating Positive Relationships: Familial Communication

Published on Singapore Psychologist #6: The Psychology of Love & Relationships, 2021

Praxis-oriented communication research in the past decade has continuously emphasised the role of effective communication on the health, happiness, and harmony of family members. This article describes some of these communication strategies that promote positive familial communication and family cohesiveness in the context of sharing both good and bad news.
